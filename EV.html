Electric cars have grown in complexity and performance since the 1880s, but the concept remains the same. A battery holds electric charge, and there is a motor connected to some or all of the wheels. As you step on the accelerator, the car provides more power to the motor propelling you forward. When you want to stop, you hit the brakes and the motor starts to slow down.
